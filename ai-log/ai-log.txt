Rubric-Driven Mock Q&A
----------------------
Q: What is the system and threat model?
A: The app embeds a `WebView` that fetches remote web content over the network. The relevant attacker is an on-path adversary on the same Wi-Fi, hotspot, proxy path, or other network segment who can observe or alter traffic between the app and the remote site.

Q: What is the exact intended Part A vulnerability?
A: The demonstrated issue is insecure transport handling. The manifest permits cleartext traffic, and the app actively loads an `http://` URL. In addition, the `WebViewClient` suppresses TLS certificate errors by calling `proceed()`.

Q: Why is this a MITM problem?
A: With cleartext HTTP, an on-path attacker can read and modify the response directly. If the app were moved to HTTPS content, the SSL-error bypass would still allow a MITM using an invalid certificate to inject or replace content.

Q: What is the realistic impact?
A: The attacker can tamper with content rendered inside the app, inject misleading or malicious material, monitor user-visible traffic, and potentially steal data entered into or displayed by insecure web content.

Q: What is the concrete mitigation?
A: Remove cleartext transport, load only `https://` endpoints, reject SSL errors instead of proceeding, and avoid permissive hostname/certificate validation. A restrictive Network Security Configuration can enforce this at the platform level.
